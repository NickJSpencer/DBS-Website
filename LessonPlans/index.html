OUR LESSON PLANS
The Dive Beneath the Surface project uses experiential learning to most effectively teach elementary school students science and engineering core concepts critical to successful STEM careers. Specifically, DBS will use marine organisms and the ocean environment to teach the Next Generation Science Standards Disciplinary Core Idea “Structure, Function, and Information Processing” to 4th graders (1). A series of three lesson plans culminates in each student learning how to “construct an argument that plants and animals have internal and external structures that function to support survival, growth, behavior, and reproduction (NGSS Performance Expectation 4-LS1-1)(1).” Each Lesson lasts about 30 minutes. Lessons 1 and 3 are conducted in the classroom, and Lesson 2 is an interactive “Live Dive” between students in the classroom and Cal Poly scientific SCUBA divers in the ocean.
Lesson 1
Lesson 1
Through an interactive experience comparing object densities in relation to water density, students explore the physical properties of water and the challenges it presents to animal functions such as movement, staying on the seafloor, and buoyancy. Next, students investigate specific structures of marine organisms (e.g., fish swim bladder and fins, kelp holdfast, sea star and urchin tube feet) and relate them to human-made objects (e.g., balloon, buoy, anchor, suction cup), in order to understand biological adaptations and conceptualize how humans have mimicked nature through engineering.
Lesson 2
Lesson 2
Using underwater audio/video technology and YouTube Live, students engage in real-time (“live”) communication with Cal Poly scientific SCUBA divers in the ocean. They pose questions to the divers and connect their knowledge of structure and function to marine life along the California central coast.
Lesson 3
Lesson 3
Students apply their knowledge of structure and function to engineering by designing, drawing and then comparing marine organisms with specific anatomical structures to support their survival, growth, behavior and reproduction.
